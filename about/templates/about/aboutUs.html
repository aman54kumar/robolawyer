About Us




Vision

JustBot aims to help applicants and their lawyers improve the quality of their applications to the European Court of Human Rights. Since 2014, when Rule 47 of the ECtHR was updated with most of its current admissibility requirements, over almost 30% of the applications processed by the Court were rejected as incomplete in the pre-judicial phase, and of those entering the judicial phase, only 5.5% reached the stage of receiving a judgement, while the large majority was declared inadmissible or has been struck out of the list. The development of JustBot is planned over several years and in several phases. In the first phase, this project provides a digital tool in English that automates the standard procedure and provides help along the way. In later phases, we aim to integrate different language versions and algorithms to automate the writing process. {% comment %} Since 2014, over 30% of the applications processed by the Court were rejected as incomplete in the pre-judicial phase, and of those entering the judicial phase, only 5% reached the stage of receiving a judgement, while the large majority was declared inadmissible or struck out of the list. Organisation

JustBot is developed by Technology for Human Rights (Tech-R) with advice and financing from the Faculty of Law at the University of Oslo (UiO) and Lovdata.

Technology for Human Rights (Tech-R) is a non-profit organisation founded in 2019 by graduates of the Norwegian Centre for Human Rights (NCHR) and Informatics Institute of the University of Oslo (UiO) . Tech-R is dedicated to finding ways of using technology for the protection and fulfilment of human rights, and this project is an important step in fulfilling our mission.

We are grateful to the University of Oslo’s Faculty of Law, Norwegian Research Center for Computers and Law (NRCCL) and Norwegian Centre for Human Rights (NCHR) for their support and involvement in JustBot. We would like to thank in particular the staff and students at the NCHR for their cooperation in testing the digital form and providing their feedback, Professor Malcolm Langford for his fundraising efforts and guidance, Ph.D. Runar Lie for his legal, technical and graphic design advice, Professor Tobias Mahler for his advice on privacy and data protection and Andra Matei, former lawyer at the European Court of Human Rights who undertook the legal quality assurance for JustBot’s digital form. We would like to extend our gratitude to Erik Møse, former judge at the European Court of Human Rights, Kjetil Mujezinovic Larsen, Professor of human rights at the Faculty of Law and Luca Tosoni, Phd. fellow in cybersecurity law at NRCCL for their contribution to the improvement of JustBots digital form. We are open to new partners joining as the project moves forward. If you are interested in collaborating with us, you can reach us by email at justbot@tech-r.org.
